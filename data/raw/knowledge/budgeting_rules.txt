The 50/30/20 budgeting rule is a widely used personal finance strategy.

50% of income should be allocated to needs such as rent, groceries, utilities, and essential expenses.
30% of income can be spent on wants such as entertainment, dining out, and non-essential purchases.
20% of income should be saved or invested for future goals.

A good budgeting strategy involves tracking expenses regularly, avoiding unnecessary debt, and maintaining an emergency fund covering at least 3 to 6 months of expenses.

Overspending on non-essential items can lead to financial instability. Individuals should aim to reduce discretionary expenses if savings fall below 20%.
