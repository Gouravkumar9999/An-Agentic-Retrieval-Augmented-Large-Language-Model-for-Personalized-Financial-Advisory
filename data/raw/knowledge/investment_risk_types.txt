Financial risks affect investments and overall financial stability.

Main types of risk:

1. Market Risk:
- Prices of stocks and assets fluctuate
- Caused by economic changes, demand, and global events

2. Credit Risk:
- Borrower may fail to repay loans or debt
- Common in bonds and lending

3. Inflation Risk:
- Rising prices reduce purchasing power
- Savings lose value over time

4. Liquidity Risk:
- Difficulty in converting assets to cash quickly

5. Interest Rate Risk:
- Changes in interest rates affect loans and investments

Rules:
- High return investments = High risk
- Low savings + High risk investments = Dangerous
- Diversification reduces overall risk

If savings are low:
→ Avoid high-risk investments

Risk must match:
- Income stability
- Savings level
- Risk preference (low / medium / high)
